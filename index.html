INTRO TO MCAI

There are two parts to the Membership examination for the College of Anaesthesiologists of Ireland.

Full details can be found on their website. The CAI publishes a comprehensive exam guide with further information.

Part 1 - MCQ

The MCQ is a 4-hour examination consisting of two papers:

  • Multiple True-False (60 Questions - 300 marks):
    • Pharmacology (20 Q)
    • Physiology & Biochemistry (20 Q)
    • Physics, Clinical Measurement & Equipment (20 Q)
  • Single Best Answer (60 Questions - 60 marks)
    • Anatomy (3 Q)
    • Pharmacology (18 Q)
    • Physiology & Biochemistry (18 Q)
    • Physics, Clinical Measurement & Equipment (18 Q)
    • Statistics (3 Q)

Each paper is 120 minutes.

Total available marks = 360

The pass mark is determined by Modified Angoff referencing.

A ‘borderline’ candidate is one who has completed the required training, has an average amount of knowledge and has done a reasonable amount of exam preparation, and who has a 50% chance of passing the exam (and 50% chance of failing).

College of Anaesthesiologists of Ireland

The exam is taken online with remote proctoring.

A pass result is valid for 3 years.

Part 2 - OSCE & SOE

The OSCE and SOE examinations can only be taken once the MCQ has successfully been passed. These exams are taken in-person.

OSCE

  • 18 stations to complete (5 minutes each)
  • 1 minute of reading time per station
  • 2 stations are trial stations (not counting towards result)
  • 1 rest station

The OSCEs cover a range of areas outlined in the MCAI curriculum, including: resuscitation; ECG; physical signs; critical incident; simulation; communication; technical skills; equipment; anatomy; airway; radiology; data; and clinical topics.

The candidate is awarded an overall "global" grade on a scale of 1-4 for the entire OSCE component following completion of scoring.

SOE

The purpose of the Structured Oral Examination is to explore both the candidate's understanding and knowledge of clinical and basic science concepts that underpin anaesthesiology and ICM, and their ability to apply this knowledge.

It is conducted in two parts of 6 questions each, with two examiners for each of the parts. There are 5 minutes given to answer each question.

Physiology & Clinical Measurement
  • Physiology & Biochemistry (4 Q)
    • Emphasis is given to cardiorespiratory physiology
  • Clinical Measurement & Equipment (2 Q)
Pharmacology & Physics
  • Pharmacology (4 Q)
    • General Pharmacology
    • Anaesthesia Pharmacology
    • Systemic Pharmacology
    • Pharmacokinetics/Pharmacodynamics/Medicinal Chemistry
  • Physics (2 Q)

The candidate is scored on a scale of 1-4 for each question by each examiner. A final grade is agreed between the examiners for each SOE.

Results

The minimum grades necessary to pass the examination are: 3, 3, 2.

A score of 1 in any part of the exam will result in an outright failure.
